Vector

October 2, 2024 · 1 min · Myrick Wang

  1. Dot Product

    • Definition: The dot product of two vectors is the sum of the products of their corresponding components, resulting in a scalar.

    • Geometric Interpretation: Can be expressed as: u·v = |u||v|cos(theta)

where theta is the angle between the two vectors.

  1. Cross Product

    • Definition: The cross product of two three-dimensional vectors produces a new vector that is perpendicular to both original vectors.

    • Geometric Interpretation: The magnitude of the cross product equals the area of the parallelogram formed by the two original vectors

  2. Magnitude of a Vector

    • Definition: The size or length of a vector, denoted as |v|. • Formula:

v = (x^2 + y^2)^1/2

For a three-dimensional vector v = (x, y, z), it can be expressed as: (x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^1/2

  1. Unit Vector

    • Definition: A vector with a magnitude of one, used to represent direction. • Formula:

u = v / |v|

where u is the unit vector, and v is the original vector.

  1. Angle Formula

    • Angle Between Two Vectors: cos(theta) = u·v / |u|·|v|
